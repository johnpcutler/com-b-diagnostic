# Avalanche Safety Personas (COM-B Framing)

Avalanche safety is a **multi-actor behavior system**. Failures often occur at the *interfaces between personas*, not just within one.

Each persona is described using:
- **Target Behaviors (B)**
- **Needs / Desires**
- **Capability (C)**
- **Opportunity (O)**
- **Motivation (M)**

---

## 1. Avalanche Forecaster

### Target Behaviors
- Produce accurate, timely, actionable forecasts  
- Communicate uncertainty clearly  
- Influence downstream decisions  

### Needs / Desires
- Credibility and trust  
- Avoid “cry wolf” fatigue  
- Professional reputation  

### COM-B

**Capability**
- Deep snow science + pattern recognition  
- Ability to synthesize incomplete signals  

**Opportunity**
- Access to field data + weather models  
- Observer network  
- Time pressure, limited coverage  

**Motivation**
- Reflective: public safety mission  
- Automatic: tension between caution vs under-warning  

### Failure Modes
- Abstract communication → low usability  
- False precision → downstream overconfidence  

---

### Needs / Desires

- Contribution to broader system

### COM-B

**Opportunity**

- Reporting tools  

**Motivation**
- Reflective: contribute to safety

---

## 3. Ski Patrol / Snow Safety

### Target Behaviors
- Execute effective mitigation  
- Make correct terrain open/close decisions  

### Needs / Desires
- Keep terrain open  
- Ensure safety  
- Operational efficiency  

### COM-B

**Capability**
- Explosives + terrain expertise  
- Pattern recognition under pressure  

**Opportunity**
- Access to mitigation tools  
- Organizational support for conservative calls  

**Motivation**
- Reflective: safety responsibility  
- Automatic: normalization of risk  

### Failure Modes
- Drift toward keeping terrain open  
- Overconfidence from repeated success

# System-Level Insights

## 1. The “Last Mile” Problem
Even with strong upstream performance:

- Forecasts are accurate  
- Data is strong  
- Mitigation is effective  

👉 Failures still occur at the **decision point (Guide / Recreationist)**

This is a COM-B breakdown:
- Capability: partial  
- Opportunity: permissive  
- Motivation: biased  

---

## 2. Motivation is the Weakest Link
Especially **automatic motivation**:
- Powder fever  
- Social pressure  
- Time pressure  
- Familiarity  

These override:
- Capability  
- Opportunity  

---

## 3. Opportunity Shapes Behavior
Environmental cues strongly influence decisions:
- Easy access → increased exposure  
- Existing tracks → perceived safety  
- Open terrain → implicit approval  

---

## 4. Misaligned Optimization Across Personas

| Persona | Optimizes For |
|--------|--------------|
| Forecaster | Accuracy + credibility |
| Patrol | Open terrain + safety |
| Guide | Experience + safety |
| Recreationist | Experience |
| SAR | Response |

No single persona owns **end-to-end behavioral alignment**.

---

# Core Framing

Avalanche safety is not a knowledge problem.

It is a **distributed behavior system** where:
- Capability is uneven  
- Opportunity is often permissive  
- Motivation is frequently misaligned  

Failure occurs when these do not align **at the moment of decision**.
