# Credits and sources

## Core models

| Model | Key authors | Role in this framework |
|-------|------------|----------------------|
| **COM-B** (Capability, Opportunity, Motivation → Behavior) | Michie, van Stralen & West (2011) | The organizing backbone: every blocker, lens dimension, and intervention traces to one of six COM-B codes |
| **Behavior Change Wheel** (BCW) | Michie, Atkins & West (2014) | Nine intervention functions that bridge COM-B diagnosis to action |
| **BCT Taxonomy v1** (93 techniques / 16 groupings) | Michie, Richardson, Johnston et al. (2013) | Named, replicable techniques that implement each BCW function |
| **PRIME Theory** | West & Brown (2013) | Motivational architecture underlying the COM-B motivation branch |

## Lens sources by COM-B branch

The four lens files deepen each COM-B branch using established research traditions. Key lineages per branch:

### Capability (`references/lenses/capability-lenses.md`)

- **Skill acquisition and procedural knowledge:** Anderson; Fitts & Posner
- **Mental models and situation models:** Johnson-Laird; Gentner & Stevens
- **Naturalistic decision-making:** Klein (recognition-primed decision); Tversky & Kahneman (heuristics and biases)
- **Signal detection and pattern recognition:** Green & Swets
- **Metacognition and calibration:** Flavell; Nelson & Narens
- **Distributed cognition and shared mental models:** Hutchins; Clark; Cannon-Bowers & Salas; Wegner
- **Scaffolding and cognitive apprenticeship:** Vygotsky; Wood, Bruner & Ross; Collins, Brown & Newman
- **Motor learning and coordination:** Fitts & Posner; Schmidt; Bernstein; Newell; Kelso
- **Physical ergonomics and work physiology:** occupational physiology, fatigue science, ICF traditions

### Motivation (`references/lenses/motivation-lenses.md`)

- **Self-Determination Theory:** Deci & Ryan
- **Self-efficacy:** Bandura
- **Expectancy-value-cost:** Eccles & Wigfield
- **Identity-based motivation:** Oyserman
- **Implementation intentions:** Gollwitzer
- **Habit formation:** Wood & Neal
- **Reinforcement and reward:** operant traditions; wanting vs liking and goal-directed vs habitual control
- **Affect and emotion:** emotion and behavior-change literatures on approach and avoidance
- **Learned helplessness:** Seligman

### Physical Opportunity (`references/lenses/physical-opportunity-lenses.md`)

- **Work-system configuration (SEIPS):** Carayon et al.; sociotechnical systems traditions
- **Workload and demand-capacity:** human-factors and operations-management traditions
- **Process architecture and resilience:** process reliability, handoff, and safety-critical domain work
- **Situation awareness:** Endsley; ecological-interface and CSE traditions
- **Affordances and usability:** Norman; ISO 9241-11
- **Physical ergonomics:** occupational human-factors traditions
- **Cybernetics and control loops:** Wiener; Ashby; systems-dynamics traditions

### Social Opportunity (`references/lenses/social-opportunity-lenses.md`)

- **Social norms:** Cialdini, Reno & Kallgren
- **Role theory and boundary work:** Kahn et al.; jurisdiction and boundary-work traditions
- **Social capital and networks:** Bourdieu; Coleman; Putnam; Burt
- **Incentives and principal-agent theory:** organizational incentive-alignment and metric-design research
- **Institutional legitimacy:** Suchman; social-identity and practice-theory traditions
- **Psychological safety:** Edmondson; organizational-politics and power-relations work
- **Viable System Model:** Beer; cybernetic governance traditions

## Original contributions

The seven situational orientation patterns, lens dimensional IDs, and product-level mechanisms are original extensions of the models above, applied to the context of organizational behavior adoption and tool design.
